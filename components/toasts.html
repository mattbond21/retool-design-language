Toasts provide brief information to users of a process that an app has performed or will perform. They appear temporarily, towards the bottom of the screen, and only one toast may be displayed at a time. They shouldn’t interrupt their experience, and they don’t require user input to disappear. Toasts can contain a single action and an optional dismiss or cancel action.

Example

  1. Short label: A short single sentence of information.
  2. One action: An action that can undo the previous operation, or similar.
  3. Long label: Information that requires two lines of text to communicate.
  4. Two ations: If appropriate, have a positive action and an optional dismiss or cancel action.

Behavior

Toasts should appear in the bottom left corner of the screen. Since they are temporary messages, it is acceptable if the toast covers important content.

Toasts should animate into position from the bottom of the screen. The movement will guide users to notice the toast, acknowledge it, and take action if necessary.
