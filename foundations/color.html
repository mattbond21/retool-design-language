The color palette is a foundation pillar of Retool. It gives the product an immediate sense of being familiar and warm. The color palette leverages Retool’s brand heritage, and builds on its sense of energy, intelligence, and confidence.

Retool is committed to complying with AA standard contrast ratios for all text in our dark theme. The color combinations in this guide don’t all comply with the AA standard for light mode, but we’re making progress towards being accessible by focusing on our dark theme.

Primary palette

Our primary palette consists of neutral grays, white, indigo, and teal. These colors are represented in the UI chrome while reserving the secondary palette when pops of energy and color are necessary.

Tips and guidelines

  • Do not simply invert a foreground or background colors for our dark theme. The variations and usage guidelines table below outlines the different ways we use color in each theme.
  • The higher a container’s elevation is, the lighter that container background becomes. This technique helps to express depth in both our light and dark themes where we use different color palettes to create structure and hierarchy.
  • The color palette has been deliberately choosen so that our dark theme is as thoughtfully designed as our light theme. For example, the 400 value secondary color palette should be switched to the 300 value in dark theme i.e. --yellow-400 in light theme should be --yellow-300 in dark theme.
  • Shadows should not be inverted to create a glow effect. Shadows should still be used in the same way as they are in light theme, they are simply more subtle as the backgrounds are darker. We have created background fills that allow a subtle depth to be created when a shadow is used.
